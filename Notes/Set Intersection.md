# Definition

> [!info] Definition 1 (Set Intersection)
> For [[Set|sets]] $A, B$
> $$
> \begin{align}
> A \cap B = \{x | x \in A \land x \in B \}
> \end{align}
> $$

Intersection of two sets includes elements that are in both sets.[^1]

> [!info] Definition 2 ([[Collection of Set]] Intersection)[^2]
> For [[Collection of Set]] $\mathcal{F}$
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \cap \mathcal{F} = \cap_{A \in \mathcal{F}} A = \{x | \forall A \in \mathcal{F}, x \in A\}
> \end{align}
> $$
> If $\mathcal{F} = \emptyset$, then $\cap \mathcal{F} = \mathcal{X}$, where $\mathcal{X}$ is the [[Universe of Discourse]]
