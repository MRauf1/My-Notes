# Review[^1]

Current LLMs seem reluctant to confess that they don't know something and would much rather confidently give out incorrect answer instead. This particularly happens in more open-ended research questions or doing state-of-the-art research for which the model doesn't have as much training data for.

Can be used for much more efficient literature review, idea discussor/proposer/improver.

LLMs sometimes need a warmup with an easier exercise before challenging them to something more difficult.

LLMs can sometimes present research that it found online without properly citing it, making the researcher potentially believe that this work is novel when it is not, so care must be taken with such cases.

LLMs are good at saving time be it for coding up manual tasks, such as visualizations, or proving simple theorems that you have an inkling are true.

You have to be an expert in your field to properly determine which LLM suggestions are actually true and correct, and when these LLMs are wrong and need more input to correct their work.

LLMs are currently limited in being used to produce novel research without proper pointers from the researcher themselves.

[^1]: [Early Science Acceleration Experiments with GPT-5](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/E6XRXLGT?page=1)
