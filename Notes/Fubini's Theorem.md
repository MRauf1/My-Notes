# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 (Fubini's Theorem for [[Double Riemann Integral]])[^1]
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \int \int_R f(x, y) dA = \int_a^b \int_c^d f(x, y) dy dx = \int_c^d \int_a^b f(x, y) dx dy
> \end{align}
> $$

The order in which you integrate doesn't matter.

> [!abstract] Corollary 2 (Corollary)
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \int \int_R g(x) h(y) dA = [\int_a^b h(y) dy] \cdot [\int_c^d g(x) dx]
> \end{align}
> $$

> [!abstract] Theorem 3 (Fubini's Theorem for [[Triple Riemann Integral]])[^2]
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \int \int_R f(x, y, z) dV = \int_a^b \int_c^d \int_e^f f(x, y, z) dx dy dz
> \end{align}
> $$
