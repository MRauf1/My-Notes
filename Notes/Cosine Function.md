# Definition

$r \cos(\theta)$ is the x-coordinate of a [[Point]] on a [[Circle]] centered at [[Origin]] with [[Radius]] $r$ that has been rotated by $\theta$ degrees counter-clockwise from the x-axis.[^1]

For a [[Right Triangle]] as depicted below, $\cos(\theta) = \frac{a}{c}$. Memorized as [[SOH-CAH-TOA]].

![[Pasted image 20260413151135.png]]

Has [[Domain]] $(-\infty, \infty)$ and range $[-1, 1]$.

> [!info] Definition 2 (Cosine Function as [[Complex Number]])
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \cos(z) = \frac{e^{iz} + e^{-iz}}{2}
> \end{align}
> $$
