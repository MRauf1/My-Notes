# Review[^1]

Even though I've never smoked, I was curious how his ideas apply more generally to any addiction. While most of the book focuses on smoking as an addiction, there are some great points about how addictions come to be and how they stay. I especially liked his analogy of a "Little Monster" that comes from the withdrawal from the addiction, and the "Big Monster" that is the mental process that keeps one stuck within the addiction. He describes well that the addiction is a mental process that is not primarily driven by the addictive substance itself, but by your own mental processes. As a result, in order to break the addiction, methods like the willpower method and cold-turkey methods won't be sustainable. Instead, one has to break the false story about the addiction that one has created in their mind and that is fueling the mental process of addiction itself. And the beauty is that once this false story has been broken, breaking the addiction becomes as easy as breathing. For you're not losing anything from withdrawing from the addictive substance.

[^1]: [Easy Way to Quit Smoking](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/AQ9ADSJ7?page=1)
