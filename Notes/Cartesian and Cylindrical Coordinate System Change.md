# Definition

> [!info] Definition 1 ([[Cartesian Coordinate System]] to [[Cylindrical Coordinate System]])[^1]
> Given a [[Point]] $P = (x, y, z)$ in [[Cartesian Coordinate System]], its representation in the [[Cylindrical Coordinate System]] is $P = (r, \theta, z)$ where $r^2 = x^2 + y^2, \tan(\theta) = \frac{y}{x}, z = z$.
> Since [[Polar Coordinate System]] has multiple representations for a point, must make sure that the $\theta$ gives a point in the same [[Quadrant]] as the [[Cartesian Coordinate System]] representation.

> [!info] Definition 1 ([[Cylindrical Coordinate System]] to [[Cartesian Coordinate System]])[^1]
> Given a [[Point]] $P = (r, \theta, z)$ in [[Cylindrical Coordinate System]], its representation in the [[Cartesian Coordinate System]] is $P = (x, y, z)$ where $x = r \cos(\theta), y = r \sin(\theta), z = z$.

[^1]: [Calculus: Early Transcendentals](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/EEFDQ9Y5?page=1072)
