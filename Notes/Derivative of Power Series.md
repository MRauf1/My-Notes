# Definition

> [!info] Definition 1 ([[Derivative]] of [[Power Series]])[^1]
> If $f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n (x - a)^n$ is [[Differentiable Function]] on the interval $I$, then
> $$
> \begin{align}
> f'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}[\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n (x - a)^n] = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{d}{dx}[c_n (x - a)^n]
> \end{align}
> $$

This only holds for power series, differentiation of other [[Infinite Series]]
 may not be like this.

Differentiation preserves the [[Radius of Convergence]], but not necessarily the [[Interval of Convergence]].

[^1]: [Calculus: Early Transcendentals](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/EEFDQ9Y5?page=786)
