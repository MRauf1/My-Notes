# Definition

> [!info] Definition 1 ([[Cartesian Coordinate System]] to [[Spherical Coordinate System]])[^1]
> Given a [[Point]] $P = (x, y, z)$ in [[Cartesian Coordinate System]], its representation in the [[Spherical Coordinate System]] is $P = (\rho, \theta, \phi)$ where $\rho^2 = x^2 + y^2 + z^2, \cos(\theta) = \frac{x}{\rho \sin(\phi)} \lor \sin(\theta) = \frac{y}{\rho \sin(\phi)}, \cos(\phi) = \frac{z}{\rho}$.
> Since [[Polar Coordinate System]] has multiple representations for a point, must make sure that the $\theta$ gives a point in the same [[Quadrant]] as the [[Cartesian Coordinate System]] representation.

> [!info] Definition 1 ([[Spherical Coordinate System]] to [[Cartesian Coordinate System]])[^1]
> Given a [[Point]] $P = (\rho, \theta, \phi)$ in [[Spherical Coordinate System]], its representation in the [[Cartesian Coordinate System]] is $P = (x, y, z)$ where $x = \rho \sin(\phi) \cos(\theta), y = \rho \sin(\phi) \sin(\theta), z = \rho \cos(\phi)$.

[^1]: [Calculus: Early Transcendentals](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/EEFDQ9Y5?page=1077)
