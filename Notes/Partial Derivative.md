# Definition

> [!info] Definition 1 (Partial [[Derivative]])[^1]
> For a [[Real-Valued Function]] $f$, its partial derivative with respect to $x_i$ at $(a_1, \dots, a_i, \dots, a_n)$ is
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i} &= f_{x_i}(a_1, \dots, a_i, \dots, a_n) \\
> &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(a_1, \dots, a_i + h, \dots, a_n) - f(a_1, \dots, a_i, \dots, a_n)}{h} \\
> &= g'(x_i)
> \end{align}
> $$
> where $g(x) = f(a_1, \dots, x_i, \dots, a_n)$
> One can obtain a partial derivative with respect to each variable of the function

Partial derivative is the [[Slope]] of the [[Tangent Line]] at the [[Point]] along a [[Curve]] that follows the variable's axis and crosses the point.
