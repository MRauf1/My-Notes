# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 ([[Fundamental Theorem of Calculus]] for [[Line Riemann Integral]])[^1]
> Let $C$ be [[Smooth Curve]] given by $r(t)$, $a \leq t \leq b$ and $f$ be [[Differentiable Function]] whose [[Gradient Vector]] $\nabla f$ (treated like [[Conservative Vector Field]]) is [[Continuous Function]] on $C$,
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \int_C \nabla f \cdot d\mathbf{r} = f(r(b)) - f(r(a))
> \end{align}
> $$
> Since $\nabla f$ is a [[Conservative Vector Field]], it is [[Line Integral Independent of Path]] by [[Line Integral of Conservative Vector Field Independent of Path]]

[^1]: [Calculus: Early Transcendentals](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/EEFDQ9Y5?page=1119)
