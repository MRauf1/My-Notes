# Definition

> [!info] Definition 2 (In [[Indefinite Riemann Integral]])[^1]
> If $x = g(t)$, where $g$ is a [[Differentiable Function]] whose [[Range]] is $I$, with an [[Inverse Function]], and with the [[Differential]] $dx = g'(x) dt$ and $f$ is [[Continuous Function]] on $I$, then
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \int f(x) dx = \int f(g(t)) g'(t) dt
> \end{align}
> $$

> [!info] Definition 2 (In [[Definite Riemann Integral]])[^1]
> If $x = g(t)$, where $g$ is a [[Differentiable Function]] whose [[Range]] is $I$, with an [[Inverse Function]], and with the [[Differential]] $dx = g'(x) dt$ and $f$ is [[Continuous Function]] on $I$, then
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \int_a^b f(x) dx = \int_{g^{-1}(a)}^{g^{-1}(b)} f(g(t)) g'(t) dt
> \end{align}
> $$

[[u-Substitution]] in reverse.
