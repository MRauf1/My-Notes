# Definition

> [!info] Definition 1 (Polar [[Coordinate System]])[^1]
> Given an [[Origin]] $O$ and a polar [[Axis]], a [[Point]] $P$ is $(r, \theta)$ where $r$ is the [[Distance]] between $O$ and $P$ and $\theta$ is the [[Angle]] between the polar axis and the [[Line]] $OP$.
> A point represented by $(-r, \theta)$ is $(r, \theta + \pi)$ and is the line opposite to $(r, \theta)$.

In this system, a single point can have multiple representations.

Works in 2D. Its 3D extensions are [[Cylindrical Coordinate System]] and [[Spherical Coordinate System]].
