# Review[^1]

Bostrom has a way with words. I'm really enjoying his prose and his use of analogies.

This is a general book on ways to achieve superintelligence, how it would play out, what risks there are, what would happen after superintelligence, the politics of all of this, and so on. It has a similarity of the underlying idea and content to that of AI2027, although this book was published in 2014, before the rise of LLMs. I wonder if this book was an inspiration for AI2027...probably.

The sections about the future of superintelligence and humanity in chapter 6, especially the parts about space colonization are particularly interesting and enlightening.

It's interesting how he keeps repeating "human's cosmic endowment" as if there is a high likelihood of us ruling over/occupying a large amount of space in the observable universe.

[^1]: [Superintelligence: Paths, Dangers, Strategies](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/9ECZCLTQ?page=1)
