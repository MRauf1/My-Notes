# Review[^1]

AI is different from previous breakthroughs since AI dives into the very core of profession including the very philosophy of the task itself rather than the process of the task. It goes down to the very foundations of the job.

The future of AI in mathematics potentially involves in formalizing their arguments to allow their proofs to go directly into formal proof assistants like Lean to verify their proofs. Once accomplished, this will streamline the process of proving new mathematical propositions. But while deductive arguments make up the core of mathematical arguments, what about more heuristic or empirical mathematical processes? Such arguments must be verified, and it is not as easy to verify AI's statements as for the deductive arguments. As such, it may increasingly be the case that the job of constructing and verifying deductive arguments/proofs will be left as a job of AI, while humans take care of the big level picture, such as which arguments to test, see how different arguments within different subfields relate to each other, the motivation behind proving certain arguments, and such. Although even this job may be taken by AI once it gets advanced enough, but the writers don't see that in the nearby future.

The one fault with such formally reasoning AI though would be that they would only solve the proofs, but may not as easily provide the motivation or the background reasoning as to why these results matter, or how they relate to other arguments, or the intuition behind these. Furthermore, sometimes the proof-writing itself can be seen as beautiful and intuition building for why the argument is true as well, which current mathematicians explore to better understand their field, but if AI simply writes down steps of logical propositions that conclude the desired proposition, this intuition may be lost or would have to be created by the human researchers.

The writers also warn that AI is trained on human data, and stepping away from that in favor of AI learning on its own could potentially lead to an AI collapse since AI doesn't learn the fundamental rules of the world, but the statistical correlations behind them. Such an environment could lead to AI becoming ungrounded from reality.

Humans value their own intellect. At first, we believed in Earth being the center of the universe, but that belief has been dispelled. We believed we were the unique and blessed creatures of God, but evolution showed that we are not as unique as we previously thought. Now, we believe that what truly differentiates us is our intellect, but even that is now under potential "threat" of being overturned by AI. As these changes happen, humans might come to the conclusion that we are not unique in the universe, but that there is also nothing wrong with that realization. Even if our intellect gets superseded, that does not imply that our intellect is now useless, as even despite the rise of AI in chess, the game of chess is still thriving and moving forward. It may be the same case with humans in general once AI surpasses human intellect.
