# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 ([[Removable Singularity]] [[Limit]] Existence Theorem)
> Let $z_0$ be [[Isolated Singularity]] of $f$. $\lim_{z \rightarrow z_0} f(z)$ exists,if and only if $f$ has a [[Removable Singularity]] at $z_0$.

> [!abstract] Corollary 2
> If $f$ has an [[Isolated Singularity]] at $z_0$ and on the [[Punctured Open Disk]] $B^*(z_0, r)$, and $|f(z)| \leq C |z - z_0|^{\alpha - 1}$ for some $\alpha > 0, C$, then $f$ has a [[Removable Singularity]] at $z_0$.
