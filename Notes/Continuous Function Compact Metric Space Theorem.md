# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 ([[Continuous Function]] [[Compact Metric Space]] Theorem)[^1]
> Given a [[Continuous Function]] $f: S_1 \rightarrow S_2$ on [[Metric Space]] $(S_1, d_1), (S_2, d_2)$, if $E \subseteq S_1$ is [[Compact Metric Space]], then
> 1) $f(E)$ is [[Compact Metric Space]] [[Subset]] of $S_2$
> 2) $f$ is [[Uniformly Continuous Function]] on $E$

> [!abstract] Corollary 2 ([[Bounded Function]])
> Let $f$ be [[Continuous Function]] [[Real-Valued Function]] on [[Metric Space]] $(S, d)$. If $E$ is [[Compact Metric Space]] [[Subset]] of $S$, then
> 1) $f$ is [[Bounded Function]] on $E$
> 2) $f$ attains its [[Minimum]] and [[Maximum]] on $E$

[^1]: [Elementary Analysis: The Theory of Calculus](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/GUY2WR3V?page=180)
