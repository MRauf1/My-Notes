# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 ([[Continuous Function]])[^1]
> If $(f_n)$ are [[Continuous Function]] and [[Sequence of Function Uniform Convergence|converges uniformly]] to $f$, then $f$ is [[Continuous Function]] and
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \int_a^b f_n(x) dx = \int_a^b \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} f_n(x) dx
> \end{align}
> $$

> [!abstract] Theorem 2 ([[Riemann Integrable Function]])
> If $(f_n)$ are [[Riemann Integrable Function]] and [[Sequence of Function Uniform Convergence|converges uniformly]] to $f$, then $f$ is [[Riemann Integrable Function]] and
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \int_a^b f_n(x) dx = \int_a^b \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} f_n(x) dx
> \end{align}
> $$
