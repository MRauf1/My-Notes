# Definition

> [!info] Definition 1 (Order of a Zero)
> Let $D$ be [[Domain (Topology)]] in $\mathbb{C}$ and $f: D \rightarrow \mathbb{C}$ [[Complex Analytic Function]] and not identically zero. Assume that $f(z_0) = 0$ for some $z_0 \in D$. Then there is a unique [[Complex Analytic Function]] $g: D \rightarrow \mathbb{C}$ and a unique $m \in \mathbb{N}$ so that $f(z) = (z - z_0)^m g(z)$ and $g(z_0) \neq 0$.
> $m$ is called the order of the zero of $f$ at $z_0$. $m = 1 \implies$ simple zero. $m = 2 \implies$ double zero.

The order of a zero of a function at point $z_0$ is the first non-zero [[Derivative]] at that point.
