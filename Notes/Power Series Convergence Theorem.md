# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 ([[Power Series]] Convergence Theorem)[^1]
> For [[Power Series]] of the form $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n (x - a)^n$
> 1) Series converges only when $x = a$
> 2) Series converges for all $x$
> 3) There is a positive number $R$ (radius of convergence) such that the series converges if $|x - a| < R$ and diverges if $|x - a| > R$. It could either converge or diverge if $|x - a| = R$. Thus, the interval of convergence is one of
> 	1) $(a - R, a + R)$
> 	2) $(a - R, a + R]$
> 	3) $[a - R, a + R)$
> 	4) $[a - R, a + R]$
