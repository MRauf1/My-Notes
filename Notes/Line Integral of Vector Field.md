# Definition

> [!info] Definition 1 ([[Line Riemann Integral]] of [[Vector Field]])[^1]
> Let $V$ be a [[Vector Field]] and a [[Smooth Curve]] $C$ given by $r(t)$, $a \leq t \leq b$, then the line integral of $V$ along $C$ is
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \int_C V \cdot d\mathbf{r} &= \int_a^b V(r(t)) \cdot r'(t) dt \\
> &= \int_C V \cdot T ds \\
> &= \int_C (P dx + Q dy + R dz)
> \end{align}
> $$
> where $V = P \cdot U_1 + Q \cdot U_2 + R \cdot U_3$
> If $V$ is a [[Conservative Vector Field]], then it is [[Line Integral Independent of Path]]

[^1]: [Calculus: Early Transcendentals](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/EEFDQ9Y5?page=1115)
