# Review[^1]

AI can present new perspectives that humans never have, and this new perspective can significantly move certain fields forward as AI fundamentally "sees" reality different from how we humans see it.

AI presents a technological shift unlike any we've seen in human history. Previous advances have created a shift, but a bounded shift. AI, on the other hand, questions the very philosophy and reality of what we're doing and ultimately can reshape all of human knowledge/experience.

First chapter is a nice and short read, while the second chapter feels like a history review. Even in this historical review part, he only focuses on Western thought.

I really like Kissinger's prose.

By the Turing test, an AI should be judged to be intelligent or humanlike based on its performance/outcome/behavior rather than the internal mechanisms by which it works (and may be completely different from humans).

Chapter 3 is also a review of some basic AI that an AI practitioner should already know.

Chapter 4 seems to be about global network platforms, or in other words, popular social media and internet applications that connect us humans together and make use of AI.

A lot of the text focuses on the geopolitical influences of AI and such, which makes sense given Kissinger's background.

Chapter 5 is more about geopolitics and treating AI as a weapon, akin to nuclear weapons, but more powerful. It talks quite a bit about topics other than AI, such as nuclear weapons and arms control and whatnot.

This book feels more targeted towards politicians rather than a technical person like me.

In addition to reason and faith as tools for understanding reality, AI will be the new addition and the third tool.

In the future, human children may be raised by AI, which would completely reshape their views of the world compared to humans today. The future children may prefer AI companionship to human companionship as AI would be much better versed in the child's liked and dislikes, preferences, and so on.

[^1]: [The Age of AI and Our Human Future](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/G5HEUC7V?page=1)
