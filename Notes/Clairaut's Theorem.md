# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 (Clairaut's Theorem)[^1]
> For a [[Function]] with a defined [[Point]] $(\mathbf{a})$, if $\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_i \partial x_j}$, $\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_j \partial x_i}$ are [[Continuous Function]],
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_i \partial x_j} = \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_j \partial x_i}
> \end{align}
> $$

The order of the partial derivatives doesn't matter as long as the numbers of each variable's partial derivatives are the same.

[^1]: [Calculus: Early Transcendentals](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/EEFDQ9Y5?page=951)
