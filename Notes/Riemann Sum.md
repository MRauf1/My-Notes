# Definition

> [!info] Definition 1 (Riemann Sum)[^1]
> Let $f$ be a [[Bounded Function]] on $[a, b]$ and $P$ a [[Partition]] of $[a, b]$. Riemann Sum of $f$ associated with the partition $P$ is
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \sum_{k=1}^n f(x_k) \cdot (t_k - t_{k-1})
> \end{align}
> $$
> where $x_k \in [t_{k-1}, t_k]$ for $k = 1, 2, \dots, n$. Choice of $x_k$ is arbitrary, meaning there are infinitely many possible Riemann sums associated with a single function and a single partition.

[^1]: [Elementary Analysis: The Theory of Calculus](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/GUY2WR3V?page=285)
