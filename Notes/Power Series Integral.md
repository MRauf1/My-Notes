# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 ([[Power Series]] [[Riemann Integral]])[^1]
> Let $f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n (x - a)^n$ with a [[Radius of Convergence]] $R > 0$. Then $f$ is integrable on $(a - R, a + R)$ and
> $$
> \begin{align}
> \int_a^x f(t) dt = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{c_n}{n + 1} (x - a)^{n + 1}, |x - a| < R 
> \end{align}
> $$
> with the same [[Radius of Convergence]].

[^1]: [Elementary Analysis: The Theory of Calculus](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/GUY2WR3V?page=220)
