# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 ([[Pole]] Order)
> Assume $f$ is [[Complex Analytic Function]] on $D$ except an [[Isolated Singularity]] at $z_0 \in D$. $z_0$ is a [[Pole]] if and only if there exists a [[Complex Analytic Function]] $h$ on $D$ such that $f(z) = \frac{h(z)}{(z - z_0)^m}$ for some $m \geq 1$ and $h(z) \neq 0$.
> $h, m$ are unique and $m$ is the order of the pole.

> [!abstract] Corollary 2 (Identifying Pole Order)
> Let $h, g$ be [[Complex Analytic Function]] on $D$ and $h$ has a zero of order $m_1$ at $z_0 \in D$ and $g$ has a zero of order $m_2$ at $z_0 \in D$. Then, if $m_1 \geq m_2$, $f(z) = \frac{h(z)}{g(z)}$ has a [[Removable Singularity]] at $z_0$ and has a zero of order $m_1 - m_2$. If $m_2 > m_1$, then $f$ has a [[Pole]] of order $m_2 - m_1$ at $z_0$.
