# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 ([[Monotonic Function]] is Riemann Integrable)[^1]
> Every [[Monotonic Function]] $f$ on $[a, b]$ is integrable. Every [[Bounded Function]] [[Piecewise Monotonic Function]] $f$ on $[a, b]$ is integrable.

> [!abstract] Theorem 2 ([[Continuous Function]] is Riemann Integrable)
> Every [[Continuous Function]] $f$ on $[a, b]$ is integrable. Every [[Piecewise Continuous Function]] $f$ on $[a, b]$ is integrable.

> [!abstract] Theorem 3 (General Properties)
> If $f, g$ are integrable functions on $[a, b]$ and $c \in \mathbb{R}$,
> 1) $cf$ is integrable with $\int_a^b cf = c \int_a^b f$
> 2) $f + g$ is integrable with $\int_a^b f + g = \int_a^b f + \int_a^b g$
> 3) $\max(f, g)$ is integrable
> 4) $\min(f, g)$ is integrable

[^1]: [Elementary Analysis: The Theory of Calculus](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/GUY2WR3V?page=289)
