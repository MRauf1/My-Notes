# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 ([[Darboux Integral]] Theorem)[^1]
> [[Bounded Function]] $f$ on $[a, b]$ is integrable if and only if for each $\epsilon > 0$, there exists a [[Partition]] $P$ on $[a, b]$ such that
> $$
> \begin{align}
> U(f, P) - L(f, P) < \epsilon
> \end{align}
> $$

> [!abstract] Theorem 2 ([[Darboux Integral]] Cauchy Criterion)
> [[Bounded Function]] $f$ on $[a, b]$ is integrable if and only if for each $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $\delta > 0$ such that
> $$
> \begin{align}
> mesh(P) < \delta \implies U(f, P) - L(f, P) < \epsilon
> \end{align}
> $$
> for all [[Partition]] $P$ of $[a, b]$.

[^1]: [Elementary Analysis: The Theory of Calculus](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/GUY2WR3V?page=283)
