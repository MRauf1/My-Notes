# Review[^1]

Book by the now deceased CMU professor [[Randy Pausch]].

I appreciate his mentality throughout part I. As he says, "the injured lion still wants to know if he can roar". Despite being diagnosed with a terminal disease, he still keeps a positive outlook and tries to achieve all of his immediate goals of setting up his family for comfort and success even without him before he passes away.

His part II on childhood dreams really shines a light on how one can achieve their childhood dreams with enough persistence, hard work, and luck. It's fascinating how many of his dreams he was able to accomplish during his adulthood. As for me, I didn't have any grand childhood dreams. I instead lived most of my childhood in the present, enjoying my time with my family and relatives. Thus, I don't have the same opportunity to pursue the same dreams as Randy. If I had to say, my current dream is to become a researcher, even though this is not technically a childhood dream.

I like the way his parents raised him. They always reinforced his curiosity about the world. They also prioritized helping other people too when they themselves were not the richest around either. This kind of mentality has built the same kind of passion within their children too.

I also appreciate part IV in which he demonstrates the power and joy of helping others realize their dreams, and how in turn, that could come back to help you and those close to you as well.

He also provides good lessons to live by in part V.
