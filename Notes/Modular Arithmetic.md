# Definition

> [!info] Definition 1 (Congruent Modulo)
> For $n \in \mathbb{N}$, $a, b \in \mathbb{Z}$ are congruent modulo $n$, denoted as $a \equiv b\ (\text{mod}\ n)$ if $n | (a - b)$, or alternatively, $tn = a - b$ for some $t \in \mathbb{Z}$
