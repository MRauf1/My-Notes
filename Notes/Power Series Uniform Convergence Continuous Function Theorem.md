# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 ([[Power Series]] [[Sequence of Function Uniform Convergence]] [[Continuous Function]] Theorem)[^1]
> Let $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n (x - a)^n$ be a [[Power Series]] with [[Radius of Convergence]] $R > 0$ (possibly $R = + \infty$). If $0 < R_1 < R$, then the power series converges uniformly on $[a - R_1, a + R_1]$ to a [[Continuous Function]].

> [!abstract] Corollary 2 ([[Continuous Function]] on [[Radius of Convergence]])[^1]
> [[Power Series]] $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n (x - a)^n$ converges (not necessarily uniformly) to a [[Continuous Function]] on $(a - R, a + R)$.

[^1]: [Elementary Analysis: The Theory of Calculus](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/GUY2WR3V?page=218)
