# Definition

> [!abstract] Theorem 1 ([[Complete Metric Space]] Theorem)[^1]
> If $(S, d)$ is a [[Complete Metric Space]], then
> 1) If $(U_n)$ is a [[Sequence]] of [[Open Set]] [[Dense Subset]] of $S$, then the [[Set Intersection]] $U = \cap_{n=1}^{\infty} U_n$ is [[Dense Subset]] in $S$ (Intersection of open and dense subsets results in a dense subset)
> 2) If $(U_n)$ is a [[Sequence]] of [[Closed Set]] [[Subset]] of $S$ and if the [[Set Union]] $U = \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} U_n$ contains a non-empty [[Open Set]], then at least one of $U_n$ also contains a non-empty [[Open Set]]
> 3) If $(U_n)$ is a [[Sequence]] of [[Nowhere Dense Subset]] of $S$, then the [[Set Union]] $U = \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} U_n$ has [[Dense Subset]] [[Set Complement]]
> 4) $S$ is not a [[Set Union]] of a [[Sequence]] of [[Nowhere Dense Subset]] of $S$

[^1]: [Elementary Analysis: The Theory of Calculus](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/GUY2WR3V?page=183)
