# Definition

> [!info] Definition 1 ([[Sequence of Function]] Uniformly Cauchy)[^1]
> [[Sequence of Function]] $(f_n)$ defined on [[Set]] $S \subseteq \mathbb{R}$ is uniformly Cauchy on $S$ if for each $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $N$ such that
> $$
> \begin{align}
> (\forall x \in S \land m, n > N) \implies |f_n(x) - f_m(x)| < \epsilon
> \end{align}
> $$

The above applies to [[Infinite Series]] by their partial sums as well.
